After graduating college, I was interested in putting my physics and geoscience degree to good use. 

Climate science and modeling looked, to me, to be an incredibly prolific data-creating machine with academics as the only end-user. I wanted to see who in the “real world” was using this data, and that led me to discover Climate Analytics. 

“Climate Modeling-as-a-Service”, “ESG Analytics”, “Risk Modeling”. A result of nascency, this field covers a lot and there are not many defined boundaries, as I found when I first start looking into them. I’m hoping this map will be a resource for others trying to figure out the different niches and major players in the field. I’m approaching this through the eyes of an interested student of climate science as well as a part-time impact VC, and hoping this will give myself a better view of the field writ large. 

I was inspired/motivated by Pietro Invernizzi’s mapping of Climate Tech Software and have used his format to structure my own effort. To repurpose his disclaimer: 

“Note: This post’s definition of [Climate Analytics] is limited to companies using software and data to [quantify] Climate Change. In a future post, it could also include other areas like [emissions tracking and carbon credit marketplaces]. Many of the startups listed sit across multiple categories. I chose one for each, for simplicity. This is not an attempt at including every single company in these categories — many companies are missing — but rather a selection of the independent, private, and usually venture-backed ones I know and find interesting.”
